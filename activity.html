The Modern Spike — the Keeling Curve

Since 1958, instruments at Mauna Loa, Hawaii have measured atmospheric CO₂ directly. This is the same quantity you just read from ancient ice — now measured in real time. The dashed gold line marks the 300 ppm natural ceiling you found in the ice-core record. Move the slider to read any year.

The comparison that matters: in the ice cores, CO₂ took roughly 10,000 years to climb the ~100 ppm from a glacial low to an interglacial high. The modern record climbed a comparable amount in well under a century.
